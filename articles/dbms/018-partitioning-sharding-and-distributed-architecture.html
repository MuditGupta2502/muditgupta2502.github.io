Partitioning, Sharding, Distributed DB Architecture, and CAP

Distributed Systems | 26 min read

Partitioning vs Sharding

  • Partitioning: split a table logically (range/hash/list) within a managed system.
  • Sharding: distribute data across independent nodes/services.

Distributed Architecture Essentials

  • Data distribution rules and shard keys.
  • Replication topology (leader-follower, multi-leader).
  • Fault tolerance and failover automation.
  • Load balancing for read/write paths.

CAP Theorem

Under network partition, systems choose between strong consistency and full availability. CAP is not about normal operation only; it defines unavoidable trade-offs in failure conditions.

ModelTrade-offTypical Fit
CPConsistency over availability during partitionFinancial correctness
APAvailability over strict consistency during partitionGlobal high-traffic services
