Locking, Timestamp Ordering, Starvation, Deadlock, and Isolation Levels

Concurrency Control | 28 min read

Concurrency Control Protocols

  • Two-Phase Locking (2PL) for serializability with lock growth then shrink.
  • Timestamp ordering protocol for deterministic conflict resolution.
  • Thomas write rule to ignore obsolete writes safely.

Blocking Risks

  • Deadlock: cyclic waiting; resolved by detection/timeouts/victim abort.
  • Starvation: one transaction repeatedly delayed; solved via fairness/aging.

Isolation Levels

LevelPreventsCan Still Happen
Read CommittedDirty readsNon-repeatable reads, phantoms
Repeatable ReadDirty + non-repeatablePhantoms (DB-dependent)
SerializableAll common anomaliesHigher contention and latency
Choose the lowest isolation level that still preserves business correctness.
